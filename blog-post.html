WHY FUNCTIONAL FITNESS BEATS THE TREADMILL

Most people join a gym and head straight for the cardio machines. But if your goal is to look better, feel stronger, and actually enjoy training — there's a better way.

CP
Chris Pinner Head Coach, Bewdley Bootcamp
Featured article image — replace with <img>

Walk into any commercial gym on a Monday morning and you'll find the same scene: rows of treadmills and cross-trainers, all occupied, all facing a wall of TVs. People grinding away at a steady pace, watching the calories tick up, waiting for it to be over.

We understand the appeal. Cardio machines feel safe. You know exactly how fast you're going, you can stop whenever you want, and nobody's watching you. But if you've been using a treadmill for six months and you still don't feel any different — there's a reason for that.

WHAT IS FUNCTIONAL FITNESS, ACTUALLY?

Functional fitness is training that improves your ability to do real things. Lift heavy objects. Move quickly when you need to. Get up off the floor without your knees complaining. Carry the shopping in from the car in one trip.

It draws on compound movements — squats, deadlifts, presses, pulls, carries — performed at varying intensities and in varying combinations. The goal isn't a number on a screen. The goal is a body that works better.

"The goal isn't a number on a screen. The goal is a body that works better."

THREE REASONS IT OUTPERFORMS STEADY-STATE CARDIO

1. It builds muscle as well as burning calories

A treadmill session burns calories during the session. Functional strength training builds lean muscle, which raises your metabolic rate 24/7. Over weeks and months, the compound effect is significant. You're not just burning more in the gym — you're burning more all the time.

2. It's genuinely varied

Boredom is one of the biggest reasons people stop training. When every session is different — different movements, different time domains, different loading — your brain stays engaged and your body keeps adapting. Adaptation is progress.

3. It transfers to real life

A stronger back. Better posture. Knees that don't ache on the stairs. The ability to play with your kids without getting winded. These are the things that actually change how you feel day to day — and they come from functional training, not the treadmill.

BUT WHAT ABOUT CARDIO FITNESS?

Here's the thing: you don't stop improving your cardiovascular fitness when you switch to functional training. Our conditioning sessions — AMRAPs, EMOMs, timed circuits — push your heart rate just as hard as steady-state cardio. Often harder. You just do it while also building strength, improving movement quality, and having a better time.

That said, steady-state cardio absolutely has its place. A long walk in the Wyre Forest, a bike ride, a swim — these are wonderful. We're not anti-cardio. We're anti-only cardio, if your goal is to change how you look and feel.

WHERE TO START

If you've never done functional fitness training before, the best first step is just to come in. Our drop-in classes are designed for all abilities — our coaches will scale movements to where you are, and you won't be left behind or pushed beyond what's safe.
